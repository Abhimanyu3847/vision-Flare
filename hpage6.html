World War I (1914-1918)

Background of the War

World War I, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict that lasted from 1914 to 1918. It involved most of the world's great powers, divided into two opposing alliances: the Allies and the Central Powers.

  • Alliance Systems: The web of alliances in Europe played a crucial role in the war's escalation.
  • Imperial Rivalries: Competition for colonial dominance added to the tensions.
  • Nationalism: Strong nationalist sentiments among nations fueled the conflict.

Causes of the War

The war was caused by a combination of political, military, and social factors, culminating in the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in 1914.

  • Militarism: Arms races and military planning created a sense of inevitable conflict.
  • Alliance Networks: Defensive treaties turned localized disputes into global conflicts.
  • Immediate Spark: The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo.

Key Battles and Events

World War I witnessed some of the most devastating battles in history, including the Battle of the Somme, the Battle of Verdun, and the Gallipoli Campaign.

  • Battle of the Somme: Marked by immense casualties, it highlighted the brutality of trench warfare.
  • Battle of Verdun: One of the longest and bloodiest battles in the war.
  • Gallipoli Campaign: A significant campaign in the Ottoman theater of the war.

Outcome of the War

The war ended with an Allied victory in 1918, leading to the Treaty of Versailles. It redrew borders, dissolved empires, and set the stage for World War II.

  • Economic Costs: Massive destruction and economic upheaval across Europe.
  • Political Changes: The collapse of empires like Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, and German.
  • Legacy: The League of Nations was established to promote peace.
